# Maryland Grade 8 Science Curriculum — Comprehensive Breakdown

### Aligned to Montgomery County Public Schools (MCPS)

*Prepared for integration into the Grade 8 app. Course: **"Investigations in Physical Science."** Last verified against MCPS + NGSS sources: July 2026.*

## 0. How to read this document

- **Bold quoted text in the standards tables is verbatim** from the official Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS), which Maryland adopted unchanged. These are the legal performance expectations.
- **"Granular concepts" and "vocabulary" lists** expand each standard into the teachable sub-ideas (drawn from the NGSS Disciplinary Core Idea elements and the published Maryland district unit descriptions). Use these as the skill nodes in the app.
- The **term-by-term plan** follows the standard Maryland four-marking-period year, mirroring the sequence used by Montgomery County Public Schools (MCPS) and Harford County Public Schools — the "Investigations in Physical Science" Grade 8 course, which is the near-universal Maryland implementation.

## 1. The one thing to understand before you build anything

Maryland does **not** have grade-by-grade science standards. In 2013 the Maryland State Board of Education adopted the **Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS)** as the Maryland College- and Career-Ready Science Standards. NGSS writes middle-school standards as a **single 6–8 grade band** — it does not tell you which standard belongs to which grade.

**Districts choose how to split the band.** Every major Maryland district (MCPS, Harford, Howard, Baltimore County, Anne Arundel, and others) uses the same **discipline-per-year model**:

| Grade | Course name | Domain taught |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| 6 | Investigations in Earth Science | Earth & Space Science (ESS) |
| 7 | Investigations in Life Science | Life Science (LS) |
| **8** | **Investigations in Physical Science** | **Physical Science (PS) + Space Systems (ESS1)** |

**Grade 8 = Physical Science, plus an astronomy/space unit** (gravity naturally bridges physics and space). Engineering Design (ETS1) is woven through every unit.

> **The assessment wrinkle you must plan for:** The **Maryland Integrated Science Assessment (MISA)** — the science component of the state's **MCAP** program (see §7) — is given in **Grade 8 (the March MISA window in MCPS)** and tests the **entire 6–8 band**: Earth (Grade 6), Life (Grade 7), *and* Physical (Grade 8). So a Grade 8 app that only teaches Grade 8 content will under-prepare students for their own state test. See §8 for the spiral-review content the app must also surface.

## 2. The three dimensions (every standard combines all three)

Every NGSS "performance expectation" (PE) is a fusion of three dimensions. Build the app so a student is never just recalling a fact — they should be *doing* a practice with a *crosscutting lens*.

1. **Science & Engineering Practices (SEPs)** — what students *do*: ask questions, develop and use models, plan and carry out investigations, analyze and interpret data, use math and computational thinking, construct explanations and design solutions, argue from evidence, obtain/evaluate/communicate information.
2. **Crosscutting Concepts (CCCs)** — the lenses that recur everywhere: patterns; cause and effect; scale, proportion & quantity; systems and system models; energy and matter; structure and function; stability and change.
3. **Disciplinary Core Ideas (DCIs)** — the content, coded by domain: **PS1** Matter, **PS2** Motion & Forces, **PS3** Energy, **PS4** Waves; **ESS1** Earth's Place in the Universe; **ETS1** Engineering Design.

## 3. Grade 8 at a glance — the four units mapped to terms

The MCPS Grade 8 course, **Investigations in Physical Science**, has **four units, taught in this exact order** (confirmed from MCPS). The **Q1–Q4 placement below is illustrative** — MCPS publishes the unit *order* but not public quarter dates, and there is a hard scheduling constraint: **the MISA is administered in March**, so the four units are **front-loaded across roughly Q1–Q3** and the Space unit (plus review) straddles the test.

| Term (illustrative) | Unit | Core standards | Big question |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Q1** | Matter and Its Interactions | MS-PS1-1 → PS1-6 | What is stuff made of, and what happens when it changes? |
| **Q2** | Wave Energy & Attractive Forces | MS-PS4-1→4-3; MS-PS2-3, PS2-5 | How do waves and invisible fields carry energy and information? |
| **Q3 (to March MISA)** | Energy, Forces & Motion | MS-PS2-1,2-2,2-4; MS-PS3-1→3-5 | Why do objects move the way they do, and where does energy go? |
| **Q3–Q4 (around/after MISA)** | A Voyage Through Space | MS-ESS1-1→1-3 (+ MS-PS2-4) | How does gravity shape the moon, the seasons, and the cosmos? |
| *All year* | Engineering Design | MS-ETS1-1→1-4 | How do we design, test, and improve a solution? |

> **Pacing implication for the app:** because MISA hits in March, all tested Grade 8 content (Units 1–3, and ideally the core of Unit 4) should be *complete or reviewable by early March*. Treat Q4 as consolidation, the space unit's tail, and engineering extension — not as new tested material introduced late.

### TERM 1 (Quarter 1) — Matter and Its Interactions

**Performance Expectations (verbatim, MS-PS1):**

| Code | Standard |
|------|----------|
| MS-PS1-1 | **"Develop models to describe the atomic composition of simple molecules and extended structures."** |
| MS-PS1-2 | **"Analyze and interpret data on the properties of substances before and after the substances interact to determine if a chemical reaction has occurred."** |
| MS-PS1-3 | **"Gather and make sense of information to describe that synthetic materials come from natural resources and impact society."** |
| MS-PS1-4 | **"Develop a model that predicts and describes changes in particle motion, temperature, and state of a pure substance when thermal energy is added or removed."** |
| MS-PS1-5 | **"Develop and use a model to describe how the total number of atoms does not change in a chemical reaction and thus mass is conserved."** |
| MS-PS1-6 | **"Undertake a design project to construct, test, and modify a device that either releases or absorbs thermal energy by chemical processes."** |

**Granular concepts (skill nodes):**
- Atoms, elements, molecules, compounds; atomic composition
- Modelling simple molecules and extended (repeating) structures
- Particle model of matter; states of matter (solid/liquid/gas) at the particle level
- Particle motion ↔ temperature ↔ thermal energy; melting, freezing, boiling, condensation, sublimation
- Physical properties as identifiers: density, melting/boiling point, solubility, flammability, reactivity, colour, odour
- Physical vs. chemical change
- Evidence a chemical reaction occurred: colour change, gas production, temperature change, precipitate, odour, light
- Conservation of mass / atoms in a chemical reaction (atoms rearrange, never disappear)
- Natural vs. synthetic materials; how synthetic materials are made from natural resources; societal/environmental impacts
- Endothermic vs. exothermic processes; designing a chemical hand-warmer / cold-pack device

**Crosscutting lenses:** Cause & Effect · Scale, Proportion & Quantity · Energy & Matter · Structure & Function
**Practices:** Developing & using models · Analyzing & interpreting data · Obtaining/evaluating/communicating information · Constructing explanations & designing solutions
**Key vocabulary:** atom, element, molecule, compound, pure substance, mixture, particle, thermal energy, physical/chemical change, chemical reaction, reactant, product, conservation of mass, synthetic, endothermic, exothermic, density, solubility

### TERM 2 (Quarter 2) — Wave Energy and Attractive Forces

**Performance Expectations (verbatim):**

| Code | Standard |
|------|----------|
| MS-PS4-1 | **"Use mathematical representations to describe a simple model for waves that includes how the amplitude of a wave is related to the energy in a wave."** |
| MS-PS4-2 | **"Develop and use a model to describe that waves are reflected, absorbed, or transmitted through various materials."** |
| MS-PS4-3 | **"Integrate qualitative scientific and technical information to support the claim that digitized signals are a more reliable way to encode and transmit information than analog signals."** |
| MS-PS2-3 | **"Ask questions about data to determine the factors that affect the strength of electric and magnetic forces."** |
| MS-PS2-5 | **"Conduct an investigation and evaluate the experimental design to provide evidence that fields exist between objects exerting forces on each other even though the objects are not in contact."** |

**Granular concepts (skill nodes):**
- Wave anatomy: amplitude, wavelength, frequency, crest, trough
- The wave model; mathematical relationship between amplitude and energy
- Mechanical waves need a medium; waves transfer energy, not matter
- What happens at a boundary: reflection, absorption, transmission (and how material affects each)
- Light and the electromagnetic spectrum (basic placement of visible light)
- Analog vs. digital signals; why digitized signals are more reliable for encoding/transmitting information
- Electric forces and magnetic forces; factors that change their strength (distance, charge/current, number of turns, etc.)
- Non-contact forces and **fields**: electric, magnetic (and gravitational) fields act at a distance; evidence that a field exists without contact

**Crosscutting lenses:** Patterns · Cause & Effect · Structure & Function · Systems & System Models
**Practices:** Using mathematics & computational thinking · Developing & using models · Asking questions · Planning & carrying out investigations · Obtaining/evaluating/communicating information
**Key vocabulary:** wave, amplitude, wavelength, frequency, medium, mechanical wave, reflection, absorption, transmission, electromagnetic spectrum, analog, digital signal, electric force, magnetic force, field, non-contact force, poles, charge

### TERM 3 (Quarter 3) — Energy, Forces, and Motion

**Performance Expectations (verbatim):**

| Code | Standard |
|------|----------|
| MS-PS2-1 | **"Apply Newton's Third Law to design a solution to a problem involving the motion of two colliding objects."** |
| MS-PS2-2 | **"Plan an investigation to provide evidence that the change in an object's motion depends on the sum of the forces on the object and the mass of the object."** |
| MS-PS2-4 | **"Construct and present arguments using evidence to support the claim that gravitational interactions are attractive and depend on the masses of interacting objects."** |
| MS-PS3-1 | **"Construct and interpret graphical displays of data to describe the relationships of kinetic energy to the mass of an object and to the speed of an object."** |
| MS-PS3-2 | **"Develop a model to describe that when the arrangement of objects interacting at a distance changes, different amounts of potential energy are stored in the system."** |
| MS-PS3-3 | **"Apply scientific principles to design, construct, and test a device that either minimizes or maximizes thermal energy transfer."** |
| MS-PS3-4 | **"Plan an investigation to determine the relationships among the energy transferred, the type of matter, the mass, and the change in the average kinetic energy of the particles as measured by the temperature of the sample."** |
| MS-PS3-5 | **"Construct, use, and present arguments to support the claim that when the kinetic energy of an object changes, energy is transferred to or from the object."** |

**Granular concepts (skill nodes):**
- Describing motion: position, distance, speed, velocity, acceleration; motion graphs (distance–time, speed–time)
- Forces: contact vs. non-contact, balanced vs. unbalanced, **net (sum of) force**
- Newton's Second Law relationship: change in motion depends on net force and mass
- Newton's Third Law: equal and opposite forces; collisions; designing a solution (e.g. bumper, helmet)
- Gravity as an attractive force that depends on mass of interacting objects
- **Kinetic energy**: how KE depends on mass and (more strongly) on speed; reading/graphing the relationship
- **Potential energy** stored in a system when the arrangement of interacting objects changes (springs, magnets, gravitational separation)
- Energy transfer and conservation; energy transferred to/from an object as its KE changes
- **Thermal energy transfer**: conduction/convection/radiation intuition; designing a device to minimize or maximize heat transfer (insulator/cooler)
- Relationship among energy transferred, type of matter, mass, and temperature change (specific-heat intuition)

**Crosscutting lenses:** Cause & Effect · Systems & System Models · Energy & Matter · Stability & Change · Scale, Proportion & Quantity
**Practices:** Planning & carrying out investigations · Constructing explanations & designing solutions · Analyzing & interpreting data · Engaging in argument from evidence
**Key vocabulary:** speed, velocity, acceleration, force, net force, balanced/unbalanced, Newton's laws, mass, gravity, kinetic energy, potential energy, energy transfer, conservation of energy, thermal energy, conduction, convection, radiation, insulator

### TERM 4 (Quarter 4) — A Voyage Through Space

**Performance Expectations (verbatim, MS-ESS1 Space Systems):**

| Code | Standard |
|------|----------|
| MS-ESS1-1 | **"Develop and use a model of the Earth-sun-moon system to describe the cyclic patterns of lunar phases, eclipses of the sun and moon, and seasons."** |
| MS-ESS1-2 | **"Develop and use a model to describe the role of gravity in the motions within galaxies and the solar system."** |
| MS-ESS1-3 | **"Analyze and interpret data to determine scale properties of objects in the solar system."** |

*(Gravity standard MS-PS2-4 is deliberately revisited here to connect physics to astronomy.)*

**Granular concepts (skill nodes):**
- The Earth–Sun–Moon system as a model
- **Lunar phases** and why they cycle
- **Eclipses** — solar vs. lunar — and the geometry that causes them
- **Seasons** caused by Earth's axial tilt (not distance from the sun)
- Gravity's role in orbits and motion within the solar system and galaxies
- Structure of the solar system; the Sun, planets, moons, asteroids, comets
- **Scale**: relative sizes and distances of solar-system objects; using data to compare
- Stars, the Milky Way, galaxies, and the scale of the universe

**Crosscutting lenses:** Patterns · Systems & System Models · Scale, Proportion & Quantity
**Practices:** Developing & using models · Analyzing & interpreting data
**Key vocabulary:** orbit, revolution, rotation, lunar phase, waxing/waning, solar eclipse, lunar eclipse, axial tilt, season, gravity, solar system, galaxy, Milky Way, astronomical scale, light-year

### ALL YEAR — Engineering Design (MS-ETS1)

Engineering is not a separate unit — it is embedded in the design tasks of Units 1 (thermal device, MS-PS1-6), 2, and 3 (collision solution MS-PS2-1; heat-transfer device MS-PS3-3).

| Code | Standard |
|------|----------|
| MS-ETS1-1 | **"Define the criteria and constraints of a design problem with sufficient precision to ensure a successful solution, taking into account relevant scientific principles and potential impacts on people and the natural environment that may limit possible solutions."** |
| MS-ETS1-2 | **"Evaluate competing design solutions using a systematic process to determine how well they meet the criteria and constraints of the problem."** |
| MS-ETS1-3 | **"Analyze data from tests to determine similarities and differences among several design solutions to identify the best characteristics of each that can be combined into a new solution to better meet the criteria for success."** |
| MS-ETS1-4 | **"Develop a model to generate data for iterative testing and modification of a proposed object, tool, or process such that an optimal design can be achieved."** |

**Design cycle to model in the app:** define problem → identify criteria & constraints → generate solutions → test & collect data → compare solutions → iterate/optimize.

## 6. Suggested app-integration mapping

A build-ready mapping (adjust lesson counts to your pacing). "Loop" = observe → attempt → feedback → master; every node must make the student *attempt* and must tell them if they were right.

| Term | Unit | PEs | Suggested skill nodes | Design task (attempt-heavy) |
|------|------|-----|-----------------------|------------------------------|
| Q1 | Matter | 6 | ~10–12 | Build/modify a hand-warmer or cold-pack (MS-PS1-6) |
| Q2 | Waves & Forces at a distance | 5 | ~9–11 | Test how materials reflect/absorb/transmit a wave (MS-PS4-2) |
| Q3 | Energy, Forces & Motion | 8 | ~12–14 | Collision-safety device (MS-PS2-1); insulator design (MS-PS3-3) |
| Q4 | Space | 3 | ~7–9 | Build a scale model of the solar system (MS-ESS1-3) |

**House-rule reminders for the build:**
- **Mix and space it.** Do not give six reps of one PE in a row. Interleave Q1 matter questions into Q2–Q4 review so nothing is quietly forgotten before the spring MISA.
- **Untimed before timed, always.** Introduce a skill untimed; only add a timer once the student is reliably correct.
- **Feedback must judge the attempt**, not just reveal the answer. Struggle → *more targeted practice*, never an easier standard.
- Because **MISA tests all of 6–8**, the app should surface periodic **spiral-review** sets drawn from §8 across the whole year — not only in Q4.

## 7. The state test — MCAP & MISA

- **MCAP (Maryland Comprehensive Assessment Program)** is Maryland's umbrella state-testing program. It covers English/Language Arts, Mathematics, **Science**, and Social Studies.
- **MISA (Maryland Integrated Science Assessment)** is the **science component of MCAP**. It is administered in **Grade 5, Grade 8, and high school** — so Grade 8 is one of the three checkpoints.
- The **Grade 8 MISA is taken in March (MCPS)** and assesses the **full Middle School NGSS band (grades 6–8)** — Earth & Space (G6), Life (G7), and Physical (G8) core ideas together. This is *why* §8 spiral review matters, and why the app must have all tested content reviewable before March.
- **Item structure:** questions come in **item sets** built around a real-world **phenomenon or scenario**. A stimulus may include text, data tables, charts, diagrams, graphics, or video. Item types are **selected response (SR)**, **technology-enhanced (TE)**, and **constructed response (CR)** — and each set includes at least one CR item where the student must write a detailed, evidence-based explanation.
- **Three-dimensional by design:** each set assesses a *bundle* of performance expectations, fusing a Science & Engineering Practice + a Crosscutting Concept + a Disciplinary Core Idea. Students must **make sense of** the given information, not just recall a fact.

**What this means for the app build:**
- Make practice **phenomenon-anchored** — present a scenario + data, then ask the student to interpret, model, or argue (not bare recall).
- Include real **constructed-response** attempts with genuine feedback, not multiple choice alone.
- **Bundle** standards the way MISA does (e.g. a wave PE paired with a data-analysis practice), and interleave Grade 6/7 review all year so the spring test isn't cold.

## 8. Spiral-review content for MISA (Grade 6 & 7 domains)

The Grade 8 MISA re-tests earlier grades. Surface light, mixed review of these so students aren't cold on their own state exam.

**Grade 6 — Earth & Space Science (taught in "Investigations in Earth Science"):**
- **MS-ESS2** Earth's Systems: rock cycle, plate tectonics, water cycle, weather & climate, ocean/atmosphere circulation
- **MS-ESS3** Earth & Human Activity: natural resources, natural hazards, human impact on the environment, global climate change
- **MS-ESS1-4** Geologic time & rock strata (Earth's 4.6-billion-year history)

**Grade 7 — Life Science (taught in "Investigations in Life Science"):**
- **MS-LS1** From Molecules to Organisms: cells, body systems, photosynthesis, sensory/information processing
- **MS-LS2** Ecosystems: interactions, energy flow, cycling of matter, biodiversity & human impact
- **MS-LS3** Heredity: genes, chromosomes, mutations, asexual vs. sexual reproduction
- **MS-LS4** Biological Evolution: fossil evidence, natural selection, common ancestry, artificial selection

## 9. Important caveats (so the app is honest)

- The Grade 8 course, unit *titles*, and **unit order** are **confirmed from MCPS** ("Investigations in Physical Science"). NGSS itself does **not** assign PEs to grades within 6–8 — MCPS's discipline-per-year split (Earth 6 / Life 7 / Physical 8) is a district decision, not a state mandate.
- The **Q1–Q4 placement is illustrative.** MCPS publishes the unit order publicly but not quarter dates; the detailed pacing guide lives behind MCPS staff login (myMCPS / Canvas). The hard fixed point is the **March MISA** — treat that as the deadline for tested content, and confirm exact quarter boundaries against your school's pacing guide.
- The **performance-expectation text is authoritative and current** (verbatim NGSS); the granular concept/vocabulary lists are a teaching-level expansion, not official standard language.
